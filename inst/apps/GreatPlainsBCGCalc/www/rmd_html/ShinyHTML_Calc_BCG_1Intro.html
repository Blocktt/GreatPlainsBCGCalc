Introduction

The MN Biological Condition Gradient (BCG) is divided into six levels of biological condition along a generalized stressor-response curve, ranging from observable biological conditions found at no or low levels of stressors (level 1) to those found at high levels of stressors (level 6) (Figure 1) (USEPA 2016).

Lists of Index Classes and links to Rules tables can be found on the bug and fish BCG subtabs. For more detailed information, see Gerritsen et al. (2017) and Gerritsen et al. (2012) on the References page.

For guidance on how to interpret the results, see the Metadata files on the Instructions page.

IMPORTANT: Data must be collected using MPCA field and laboratory protocols (see MPCA 2017 reports on the References page).
